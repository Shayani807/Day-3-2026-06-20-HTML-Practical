Saman

ICET

My name is less important than the kind of person I am. I’m 21 years old, living in Colombo, and I’m at that exciting stage of life where everything feels like a beginning. I’m curious by nature. From a young age I’ve loved learning new things and trying them out, even if I fail at first. That’s why I’m now exploring HTML, CSS, and coding. When my code didn’t run and I said “Output eka enne na ne”, I didn’t give up. I asked questions, fixed mistakes, and learned. That “try again” attitude is a big part of me. I’m a talkative, cheerful person. I believe life becomes lighter when you can laugh at problems instead of crying over them. My friends say I bring energy to the group. I’m the one who cracks jokes when things get tense and who reminds everyone that “it’ll be okay”. I also care deeply about people. If I can help someone with even a small thing, like explaining a concept or listening to their problems, I’ll do it. For me, kindness is not a big gesture. It’s in small daily actions. I’m ambitious, but my dreams are simple and real. I don’t chase fame or just money. I want to study well, build a career I love, and make my parents proud. I want financial independence, yes, but more than that I want peace of mind. At the end of the day, if I can sleep knowing I gave my 100% and didn’t hurt anyone, that’s success for me. At 21, I’m not afraid to make decisions. Whether it’s choosing a course, deciding to get a cartilage piercing, or picking a career path, I research, think, and take the step. If I fail, I learn. If I win, I celebrate. That’s life. Of course I have flaws. I’m a bit of a procrastinator sometimes. “I’ll do it tomorrow” is a line I use too much. I also overthink small things. My mind can run 10 steps ahead and create problems that don’t exist yet. But I’m working on it every day. I don’t aim to be perfect. I just want to be better than who I was yesterday. Progress, not perfection, is my motto. I love simple things. Reading, listening to music, watching the night sky, and random road trips to the beach. Being in Colombo means I have the ocean close by, and nothing clears my head like sea breeze and waves. I’m proud of my country, our food, our culture, and our people. Sri Lankan hospitality and resilience inspire me every day. In short, I’m a normal 21-year-old girl with big dreams, a learning mindset, love for my people, and a light heart. I’m still figuring things out, still growing, still making mistakes and fixing them. 21 is just the start of my story. The pages ahead will have tears, laughter, failures, and wins. But I want to be remembered as a good daughter, a loyal friend, and a decent human being. I’m me, and I’m proud of that. I’m still writing my story, one chapter at a time.
